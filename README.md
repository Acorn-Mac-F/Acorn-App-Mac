## Working With Layers

Layers are the heart of the <a href="#">Acorn app Mac</a>, and using them well is the key to
professional results:

1. **Add a layer** for each new element so photos, text, and shapes stay separate and editable.
2. **Reorder layers** by dragging them in the layers palette to control what sits in front.
3. **Set blend modes** to change how a layer combines with those beneath it for lighting and color
   effects.
4. **Add a layer mask** to hide or reveal parts of a layer with a brush for seamless blending.
5. **Group layers** to organize complex documents and move related elements together.

Mastering this layer workflow in <a href="#">Acorn Mac</a> unlocks everything from simple touch-ups
to elaborate photo composites.

## Exporting and Saving From Acorn

When your edit is complete, <a href="#">Acorn Mac</a> exports to the format you need:

your working master.

graphics and transparency; JPEG suits photos where smaller file size matters.

from the Acorn app for blogs and social media.

## Key Terms

**Layer** — a separate level holding one image element, editable independently of the rest.

**Non-destructive editing** — applying filters and adjustments without permanently changing the
original pixels.

**Blend mode** — how a layer's colors mix with the layers below it.

**Layer mask** — a grayscale overlay used to hide or reveal parts of a layer.

**Export** — saving a finished image to a shareable format such as PNG or JPEG.

**Flatten** — merging all layers into a single image for final output.
